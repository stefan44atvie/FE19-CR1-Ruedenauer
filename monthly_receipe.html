Preparation

  1. Spread a little butter on a slice of toast and top with two slices of ham so that the entire toast is covered. You may have to cut the ham to the right size to do this.

  2. Then cover the ham well with cheese, but make sure that it does not protrude over the edge of the toast (otherwise this cheese would melt too soon and drip into the pan). Sprinkle with a little salt and pepper and place the rest of the ham on top.

  3. Now place the second slice of bread on top, press down lightly and spread a little butter on top.

  4. Melt a teaspoon of butter in a small skillet (with a lid) and place the toast, non-buttered side down.

  5. Now fry for 1-2 minutes over medium heat until it has turned golden. Then turn with a spatula and let it turn golden again for 1-2 minutes.

  6. Reduce the heat to the absolute minimum, put the lid on and toast each side for about 6-7 minutes more. After a total of about 15 minutes, the toast should be really crispy and the cheese should be nicely melted on the inside.

  7. Lift out and wrap halfway in a napkin, which can then be folded over like a bag.

  8. Serve with ketchup to taste.
